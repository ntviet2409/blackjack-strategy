Step 1 — Learn the Rules

Everything you need to know before sitting at a blackjack table.

The Goal

Beat the dealer by getting a hand value closer to 21 without going over (busting). You are NOT trying to get 21 — you are trying to beat the dealer.

Card Values

CardValueExample
2 – 10Face value7 of hearts = 7
Jack, Queen, King10Queen of spades = 10
Ace11 or 1A + 6 = 17 (soft)  |  A + 6 + 8 = 15 (hard, Ace = 1)
Hard vs Soft Hands — This Is Critical

Soft hand: Contains an Ace counted as 11 (e.g., A-6 = soft 17). Safe to hit — the Ace drops to 1 if you'd bust.
Hard hand: No Ace, or Ace already counts as 1 (e.g., 10-7 = hard 17, or 10-5-A = hard 16). Hitting risks busting.

Why does this matter? The strategy is completely different. Soft 17 = hit or double. Hard 17 = always stand. Same number, opposite action.

Your 5 Options

Hit (H)

Take another card. You can keep hitting until you stand or bust (go over 21).

Stand (S)

Keep your current hand. Your turn is over and the dealer plays.

Double Down (D)

Double your bet, receive exactly ONE more card, then stand. Best when you have 10 or 11.

Split (P)

Only when you have a pair (e.g., 8-8). Split into two separate hands, each with its own bet.

Surrender (R)

Give up half your bet and fold immediately. Only on your first two cards. Not offered at all tables.

Dealer Rules — No Choices, Just Rules

The dealer plays mechanically. This is what makes basic strategy work — you know exactly what the dealer will do.

1Dealer must hit on 16 or less — no exceptions
2Dealer must stand on 17 or more (most tables)
3Dealer's first card is face-up (the "upcard") — your entire strategy is based on this one card
4Dealer's second card is face-down (the "hole card") — revealed only after you finish

The 3 Key Principles

Before memorizing charts, understand WHY basic strategy works:

1 Dealer 2–6 = Weak (likely to bust). The dealer must hit, and with a weak upcard, they often draw to over 21. Stand on your stiff hands (12–16) and let them bust. Don't risk busting yourself trying to improve.
2 Dealer 7–A = Strong. The dealer likely already has 17+. Your 14 or 15 won't win, so hit to improve — even though you might bust. Doing nothing loses more often.
3 Double when the math favors you. When you have 10 or 11 and the dealer is weak, your most likely next card is a 10-value (there are 16 of them in a deck). Double your bet — the odds are in your favor.
What is "Basic Strategy"?

It's the mathematically optimal decision for every possible hand. Developed by running millions of computer simulations. Following it perfectly reduces the house edge to about 0.5% — the best odds of any casino game. It doesn't guarantee you'll win every hand, but over thousands of hands, you'll lose the least amount possible.

How to Read the Chart

Row = your hand total. Column = dealer's face-up card. Find your row, find the dealer's column, and the cell tells you what to do. Tap or hover any cell for a description. On small screens, swipe the table sideways to see all dealer cards.

Hard Totals — Memorize These First
  • 8 or less: Always hit — you can't bust and any card improves your hand
  • 9: Double vs dealer 3–6, otherwise hit
  • 10: Double vs dealer 2–9, hit vs 10 or Ace
  • 11: Always double — the best doubling hand in blackjack
  • 12: Stand vs dealer 4–6 only, hit everything else (this catches people!)
  • 13–16: Stand vs dealer 2–6 (weak), hit vs 7+ (strong)
  • 17+: Always stand — never hit a hard 17 or higher

You have an Ace counted as 11. These hands are flexible — you can't bust with one more card.

Soft Totals — Memorize These First
  • Soft 13–14 (A-2, A-3): Double vs dealer 5–6 only, otherwise hit
  • Soft 15–16 (A-4, A-5): Double vs dealer 4–6, otherwise hit
  • Soft 17 (A-6): Double vs dealer 3–6, otherwise hit — never stand on soft 17!
  • Soft 18 (A-7): The trickiest hand. Double vs 2–6, stand vs 7–8, hit vs 9–A
  • Soft 19–20: Always stand — these are strong hands

Two cards of the same value. Splitting creates two hands, each with its own bet.

Pairs — Split or Don't Split
  • Always split Aces: Each Ace + a 10-value card = 21. Two chances to win big
  • Always split 8s: 16 is the worst hand in blackjack. Two 8s is a fresh start
  • Never split 10s: You already have 20! Don't ruin a great hand
  • Never split 5s: You have 10 — double down instead
  • Split 2s, 3s, 6s, 7s: Only vs dealer 2–6 (weak dealer)
  • 9s: Split vs 2–6 and 8–9. Stand vs 7 (your 18 beats their likely 17), stand vs 10/A
